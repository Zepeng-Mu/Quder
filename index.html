QUDER Qubit with plate reader

Qubit Quantification Protocol

Well Preparation
  • In each well: 98µl buffer solution + 2µl sample (or standard)
  • If using 384-well plate: 39µl + 1µl sample (or standard)
  • Buffer Solution: use 1x HS DNA buffer
Standards

Serial dilution to make 8 standards in a column of a 96w plate or 8-strips.

  • 8 standards: 10, 5, 2.5, 1.25, 0.625, 0.3125, 0.156, 0
  • Start with 25µl of S1 (0) in each well except the first one.
  • Add 50µl of S2 (10) in first well.
  • Take 25µl from the first well and transfer to the next well. Mix well! Transfer 25µl from the second well to the third well, etc. Leave tube 8 with only the S1.
  • SAVE until runs out or R² is not less than 98.
Plating & Reading
  • Pipette first the buffer solution to each well you will use.
  • Recommendation: I usually use a 384w plate and put samples into every other column using a multichannel pipette. I use the lower rows N, O & P for the standards, and pipette those in triplicate.
  • Vortex plates VERY VERY well and read in Synergy H1 Hybrid Reader, using Qubit_384w_protocol.
Quantification with the Calculator

Switch to the Calculator tab to fit a standard curve and predict sample concentrations.

  1. Set dilution factors in the sidebar — Standard dilution factor (default 5) and Sample dilution factor (default 2) must match how you prepared your plate.
  2. Enter standard readings in the Standard Curve Data table. Type or paste the raw fluorescence values from the plate reader into the Reading column.
  3. Click Fit Standard Curve. Check the R² badge — aim for ≥ 0.99 (Excellent). If R² is poor, review your standard readings and check for pipetting errors.
  4. Enter sample readings in the Sample Results table. Predicted concentrations and pool volumes populate automatically.
  5. Set your Target pooling mass (ng) in the sidebar to adjust pool volumes for all samples at once.
  6. Click Export CSV to download results, or Add Sample to append rows manually.
  7. Optionally, upload a CSV/TXT file exported from the plate reader software via the Import panel — the app auto-detects separators and plate layout.
Notes
  • Stay within the standard curve range. The highest standard is 10 ng/µl — dilute samples to stay below this if possible. Extrapolating beyond the curve is allowed but increases the risk of inaccurate predictions outside the linear dynamic range.
  • Avoid saturating the plate reader. Raw readings above ~40,000 exceed the instrument's physical limit and will produce unreliable results. Dilute any samples or standards that approach this threshold.
